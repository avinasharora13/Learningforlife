How it works

Two modalities, three phases

You can follow the Complete Process (from first analysis to post-admission) or, if you already know your destination, start directly with the Direct Plan.

Phase 0 – Informative session (free)
Before starting, we hold a free session to explain the program, answer questions and confirm whether your profile fits international options.
Phase 1 – Start of the process
Signature of the contract, payment of the first fee and analysis of your academic record, language level, interests and goals. At the end we deliver a personalized dossier with recommended programs and universities.
Phase 2 – Application management
Preparation and submission of applications to up to three institutions: forms, motivation letters, documents and administrative follow-up. We track deadlines and university responses with you.
Phase 3 – Post-admission and visa support
Once admitted, we help with place confirmation, guidance on accommodation, and orientation for the visa process if required. There is also the option to add full visa handling as an extra service.
Complete Process vs Direct Plan

Complete Process. Recommended if you want to start from scratch and receive support from the first analysis to enrollment. Includes Phases 1, 2 and 3, each with its own payment and scope.

Direct Plan. Designed for students who already know which universities and programs they want. There is no Phase 1: we verify requirements, manage applications (up to three, or a reduced rate for a single application) and accompany you after admission.

Prices change depending on whether the destination is within the European Union or outside the EU / in Health fields (Medicine, Dentistry, Veterinary). Each table of the guide details the amounts per phase and modality and always includes VAT.

Elite & Elite Plus supplements for top universities
Selective universities

Elite and Elite Plus applications

Some universities and programs require an extra level of preparation, essays and interview work. For them, the guide defines specific supplements.

Elite
Top-100 and highly selective programs

Applied when the university is in the Top-100 of rankings such as QS, THE or ARWU, or when the program has extra competitive requirements (specific tests, intensive essays or interviews).

Includes additional reviews of your main essay, a complete check of the application portal before submission and more personalized support.

Supplement: around +€600 per application
Elite Plus
Oxbridge, Ivy+ and similar

For universities such as Oxford, Cambridge, Ivy League schools, MIT or Stanford, where admissions are extremely selective and workload per application is much higher.

The Elite Plus supplement adds more essay work, strategic planning, mock interviews and close follow-up across all phases.

Supplement: around +€900 per application
How we apply them
Transparency from the beginning

In the options list you receive at the end of Phase 1, any university or program that requires Elite or Elite Plus will be clearly identified. Supplements are always added on top of the base service and applied per application.

If you add a 4th application that is also Elite, both supplements (4th-application + Elite/Elite Plus) are applied according to the guide.

Planning

Typical timeline from first call to departure

Exact dates vary by country and program, but this is a realistic reference for how the process unfolds when we work together.

Week 0 – Informative session
First free meeting to understand your interests, academic goals and preferences. We explain how the service works and confirm if it suits your case.
Weeks 1–3 – Phase 1
We collect documentation, analyze your profile and prepare a dossier with recommended options, including descriptions, approximate costs, admission requirements and key dates. You choose which paths to follow.
Weeks 4–7 – Phase 2
Preparation and submission of applications: forms, motivation letters or personal statements, CV, references and specific requirements for each institution.
After submission – University responses
Response times vary widely. Some universities reply in a few weeks; others take several months, and some may ask for additional documents, tests or interviews.
8–12 weeks before travel – Visa & preparations
Once admitted, we recommend starting the visa process at least two or three months before departure (often more). We help you organize visa, accommodation, insurance and flights.
1–4 weeks before traveling – Final checks
We ensure that enrollment, visa, insurance and accommodation are all confirmed. You receive guidance on arrival, academic induction and first steps at the destination.
Timing tips

· If your destination requires a visa, plan with 6–8 months of lead time whenever possible.
· For Elite universities, add 4–6 extra weeks for essays and interviews.
· The slowest part is usually collecting documents, so it helps to start early.
· Avoid paying non-refundable flights until you have admission (and visa, where necessary).

The guide also includes approximate key dates by destination (UK, Netherlands, EU, Canada/USA, etc.) so you can see if you are early, on time or late relative to typical windows.

Security & protection

Insurance and cancellation options

Studying abroad is a major investment. The guide explains which insurances are mandatory, which are optional and which risks they cover.

Medical & travel
Health, assistance and liability

For non-EU destinations, medical and travel insurance with specific coverage is generally mandatory. Within the EU, it is strongly recommended and sometimes required by universities.

Typical coverage includes international medical assistance, hospitalization and emergencies, repatriation, liability and accident insurance. We can provide quotes adapted to your destination and length of stay.

Included as part of your personalized quote
Optional cancellation
Standard and Premium options

Cancellation insurance is optional but especially useful for long programs or those requiring a visa. It covers justified causes (serious illness, accident, death of a family member, official summons, etc.) depending on the chosen modality.

The guide explains different insured sums, prices and the main differences between Standard and Premium: flexibility, higher insured amounts and suitability for long stays.

Our fees do not include university fees (applications, deposits, enrollment), sworn translations, official exams (IELTS/TOEFL, SAT/ACT, TSA/LNAT/UCAT/BMAT, etc.), visa fees, external teachers or mentors, courier costs or other external administrative procedures.

These costs, when necessary, are quoted separately or paid directly to the institution or provider.

Questions

Key points from the conditions

The Enrollment Guide includes a detailed FAQ. Here are some of the questions that most families and students ask before starting.

What does “EU” and “outside the EU” mean in the fees?
For our service, “EU” refers to countries in the European Union. The United Kingdom and other non-member countries are considered outside the EU. If the applicant does not have an EU nationality, some fee rules may not apply in the same way.
What exactly is a “standard application”?
A standard application is a direct application to a university or program without the intensive requirements of Elite or Elite Plus cases (for example, multiple essays, specific tests or mandatory interviews).
If I apply through a centralized portal, does it count as several applications?
No. If a centralized portal allows you to choose several options within the same platform, it counts as a single application for service purposes. We will ask you to prioritize your choices.
When will I know if my case is Elite or Elite Plus?
At the end of Phase 1 you receive a list of recommended options. In that document we clearly mark which universities or programs require an Elite or Elite Plus supplement, so you can decide with full information.
How and when are the phases paid?
You pay by stages: Phase 1, then Phase 2, then Phase 3. You only move on to the next phase if you wish to. Each payment corresponds to services that are clearly defined and documented in the guide and contract.
Are phases or external fees refundable?
Work already performed in a phase is not refundable. University fees, visa fees and other external costs are governed by each institution’s policy; we will explain their conditions before you pay them.
